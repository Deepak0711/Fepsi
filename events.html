Events

Pragathi

Every Voice Counts...



The attempt for a world was conducted to raise our voices against the brutal gang rape of Delhi Girl Jyoti Singh on 4th February 2013. Over 3005+ students joined together and stood in the form of a giant human hand breaking the existing Guinness world record for the largest human image of the hand by Nestle Puerto Rico. The event was witnessed and acknowledged by the Chancellor of VIT, Dr. G. Viswanathan, executives from Indian Bank, Chief Guests and executive officials from the government of India. Pragathi Record was covered by national newspapers like The Hindu, The Deccan Chronicle and was recognized and validated by The World Record Academy, USA.

VIT Aid

Not Just A Fund Raiser...



A fund raising campaign in VIT University organised by FEPSI along with VIT was held on July 2013 to aid the flood victims of the Uttarakhand disaster in June 2013. It was a mass gathering of supporters, which lead to a collection of Rs.40 lakhs in the University campus. The Vice President of VIT, Mr. Sankar Viswanathan along with the Vice Chancellor Prof. V. Raju, handed over the Demand Draft of above mentioned amount collected to Uttarakhand’s (then) Chief Minister Shri Vijay Bahuguna.

Maker's Fete

You Can't Put A Price Tag on Knowledge...



“MAKERS FÊTE” offered the opportunity for students to see themselves as more than consumers; we are productive; we are creative. There is a Maker in every one of us. MAKERS FÊTE‟14 was a platform for all change makers in the country to showcase and display change making ideas, creativity, inventions, resourcefulness and start-ups that in anyway help in making the world a better place to live in. Makers Fête was a gathering of technology enthusiasts, crafters, tinkerers, hobbyists, engineers, science clubs, students, and commercial exhibitors. All of these “Makers” were welcomed to Makers fête to showcase their ideas and to share what they have learned and experienced.

Socon

Revolution Is Yet To Come...



Social Convivial offered a platform to all artisans, craftsmen, economists, engineers and other enthusiasts who were willing to show their resourcefulness towards such a grass root level development project. It was a scaffold for all such enthusiasts to showcase their talents and bring about a revolutionary change in our society. SoCon aimed on popularizing the idea of revolutionizing our society through development of our primary sector. Increasing the national income of our country by enhancing mechanisms in agriculture is old school, we should remember that factors such as basic sanitation, basic healthcare and other such amenities play a pivotal role in aspects of rural development. We should deliberately consider these and act accordingly. SoCon focused on such novelty, which people on the higher scale of our society tend to ignore.

Umang

A Ray of Hope...



Umang was organised by FEPSI as a Campaign Against Substance Abuse (CASA) at Greenos in the year 2017 using entertainment as the means to spread awareness among the youth. It incorporated open mic, dance, music, shaayari and many such activities each of which were used as a platform to convey the message to the youth as a campaign against substance abuse and informing them about the ill-effects of substance abuse through these performances. With over 70 active participants and a crowd of over 120 inside Greenos and many spectators around the periphery, we successfully conveyed a strong message against substance abuse by means of entertainment.
